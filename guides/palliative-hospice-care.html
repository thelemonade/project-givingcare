Palliative Care and Hospice Care

Understand comfort-focused care and when to ask for additional support.

9 min read

Before you begin

Use this guide as a starting point. Individual needs differ, and service eligibility or legal requirements may change.

What palliative care means

Palliative care focuses on quality of life and relief of physical, psychological, social and spiritual distress. It can be provided alongside treatment intended to control disease or prolong life.

Support may address pain, breathlessness, nausea, fatigue, anxiety, family communication and future care planning.

What hospice care means

Hospice services generally support people with serious, life-limiting illness and their families. Care may be delivered at home, in inpatient hospices, day services, hospitals or through consultative teams depending on needs and eligibility.

Hospice care does not mean that care stops. The focus shifts towards comfort, dignity, meaningful time and support for the family.

When to ask for a referral

Ask when symptoms are difficult to control, hospital visits are increasing, the illness is progressing, family members are unsure what to expect or the person wishes to focus more on comfort.

Early referral allows time to build trust, discuss goals and prepare for emergencies.
